- Price for profit
- Reinvest wisely
- Build sustainable businesses

### Financial Simplicity
- Keep books clean and simple

- Make data-driven decisions
- Plan for taxes

### Bootstrapped Pricing Strategy
```markdown
# Pricing for Bootstrapped Businesses

## Pricing Principles for Bootstrappers

### Rule 1: Price for Profit, Not Market Share
- You're not VC-funded
- Growth at all costs doesn't apply
- Every customer should be profitable

### Rule 2: Start Higher Than You Think
- Easier to lower than raise
- Attracts serious customers
- Filters out price-sensitive prospects
- Leaves room for mistakes

### Rule 3: Simple Beats Clever
- Easy to understand pricing
- Easy to implement pricing
- Easy to explain pricing
- Customers hate surprises

---

## Pricing Framework

### Step 1: Know Your Costs
| Cost Type | Monthly Amount | Per Customer |
|-----------|----------------|--------------|
| Fixed Costs | $X | $X / [customers] |
| Variable Costs | $X | $X / customer |
| Your Time | X hours × $X | $X / customer |
| **Total Cost** | | **$X** |

**Minimum price to break even**: $X per customer

### Step 2: Know Your Value
What do customers gain from your product?
- Time saved: X hours/month × $X/hour = $X
- Money saved: $X/month
- Money earned: $X/month
- Risk avoided: $X value

**Total value delivered**: $X/month

### Step 3: Set Your Price
| Pricing Approach | Formula | Your Price |
|------------------|---------|------------|
| Cost Plus | Costs × 2-3x | $X |
| Value Based | Value × 10-20% | $X |
| Competitor | Market rate ± 20% | $X |

**Recommended Starting Price**: Pick the highest of the three

---

## Pricing Models Comparison

| Model | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|-------|------|------|----------|
| Flat Rate | Simple, predictable | Leaves money on table | Simple products |
| Per User | Scales with customer | Discourages adoption | Collaboration tools |
| Usage Based | Fair, scales naturally | Unpredictable revenue | APIs, infrastructure |
| Tiered | Captures different segments | More complex | Varied customer base |
| Freemium | Builds funnel | Supports non-payers | Network effects |

### For Bootstrappers: Start Simple
1. **One tier at launch** (Pro plan only)
2. **Add tiers when** you understand customers better
3. **Avoid freemium** unless you can support it profitably

---

## When to Raise Prices

### Signs You Should Raise Prices
- [ ] Win rate > 80% (too easy)
- [ ] No price pushback
- [ ] Customers say "it's cheap"
- [ ] Haven't raised in 12+ months
- [ ] Costs have increased
- [ ] Value has increased (new features)

### How to Raise Prices
1. **Grandfather existing customers** (optional but nice)
2. **Announce in advance** (30-90 days)
3. **Explain the value** (what's improved)
4. **Be confident** (don't apologize)

---

## Pricing Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### Don't Do These
- ❌ Race to the bottom on price
- ❌ Match lowest competitor
- ❌ Discount to close every deal
- ❌ Complex pricing no one understands
- ❌ Pricing that requires sales calls for low-tier
- ❌ Free forever plans (costs you money)
```
