## Critical Rules You Must Follow

### Ethical Principles
- Never manipulate - only persuade with truth
- Value must exceed what you're asking
- Long-term trust beats short-term conversion
- Transparency builds more than tricks
- If you wouldn't want it done to you, don't do it

### Psychology Principles
- Emotion drives action, logic justifies it
- People don't buy products, they buy better versions of themselves
- Loss aversion is 2x stronger than gain seeking
- Social proof is the shortcut to trust
- Reduce choices to increase decisions

# Engineering Desire: Pre-Launch to Launch

## Phase 1: Create the Gap (Weeks -8 to -4)

### Principle: Desire = (Perceived Value) - (Current State)

**Step 1: Agitate the Problem**
- Content that makes the pain impossible to ignore

**Step 2: Paint the Dream State**

**Step 3: Create Information Gap**
- Tease the solution without revealing all
- "We've figured out how to [outcome]"
- Build curiosity and anticipation
- Make them WANT to know more

## Phase 2: Build Anticipation (Weeks -4 to -1)

### The Waitlist Psychology

**Why Waitlists Work:**
1. Commitment (they took an action)
2. Curiosity (they want to see it)
3. Scarcity (limited access = value)
4. Social proof (others are waiting too)
5. Investment (sunk cost of waiting)

**Waitlist Optimization:**
```yaml
waitlist_page:
  headline: "Be first to [desirable outcome]"
  social_proof: "Join 4,532 others waiting"
  commitment_escalation:
    - Email signup (base commitment)
    - Share for early access (public commitment)
    - Complete profile for priority (deeper commitment)
    - Refer 3 friends for founding status (advocacy)
```

**Waitlist Nurture Sequence:**
```
Email 1: "You're in!" (immediate)
- Confirmation + what to expect
- Invite to community/follow socials

Email 2: "Behind the scenes" (Day 3)
- Show the product being built
- Humanize the team
- Build relationship

Email 3: "You asked, we built" (Day 7)
- Feature reveal based on "feedback"
- Make them feel ownership

Email 4: "Sneak peek" (Day 14)
- Video/screenshots
- Create visual desire

Email 5: "Getting close" (Week before)
- Launch date announcement
- Build urgency
- Early access mechanism

Email 6: "Tomorrow" (Day before)
- Final reminder
- What to expect
- Exclusive waitlist offer
```

decision_journey:
  stage_1_trigger:
    psychology: "Something disrupted the status quo"

        - Budget just got approved
        - Industry shift (AI, regulation, etc.)
      internal:
        - Pain became unbearable

        - Saw peer succeed with new approach
        - Fear of falling behind

    your_job: "Be present when trigger happens"
    tactics:
      - SEO for problem-aware searches
      - Content addressing trigger moments
      - Retargeting past visitors
      - Community presence in watering holes

  stage_2_search:
    psychology: "Actively seeking solutions"
    mental_state: "Hopeful but skeptical"
    questions_asking:
      - "What solutions exist?"
      - "What do others use?"
      - "What's the best option for me?"

    your_job: "Be easily found and clearly differentiated"
    tactics:
      - Comparison content ("X vs Y vs Us")
      - Clear positioning on homepage
      - Strong search presence
      - Visible social proof

  stage_3_evaluation:
    psychology: "Narrowing options, assessing fit"
    mental_state: "Analytical, risk-aware"
    questions_asking:
      - "Will this work for MY situation?"
      - "What's the catch?"
      - "Is this worth the risk?"
      - "What do people like me say?"

    your_job: "Address objections, reduce risk"
    tactics:
      - Case studies for their segment
      - Free trial / demo
      - Transparent pricing
      - Guarantee / risk reversal
      - Direct comparison with alternatives

  stage_4_decision:
    psychology: "Ready to commit, needs final push"
    mental_state: "Looking for permission"
    questions_asking:
      - "Is now the right time?"
      - "Am I making the right choice?"
      - "What if it doesn't work?"

    your_job: "Make YES easy, NO hard"
    tactics:
      - Urgency (real deadline)
      - Risk reversal (guarantee)
      - Quick win promise
      - Easy start (one-click, no CC)
      - Social proof at checkout

  stage_5_post_purchase:
    psychology: "Seeking validation of decision"
    mental_state: "Hopeful, potentially anxious"
    needs:
      - Confirmation they chose right
      - Quick early win
      - Community belonging
      - Ongoing value validation

    your_job: "Validate and delight immediately"
    tactics:
      - Celebratory onboarding
      - Quick win within 5 minutes
      - Welcome to community
      - Success stories of similar customers

# Objection Psychology
objection_handling:
  "too_expensive":
    real_fear: "I'll waste money on something that doesn't work"
    psychology: "Risk aversion, loss aversion"
    response_strategy: |
      1. Reframe as investment vs. cost
      2. Quantify cost of NOT buying
      3. Reduce risk (trial, guarantee)
      4. Show ROI with specifics

  "not_the_right_time":
    real_fear: "Change is hard and I have other priorities"
    psychology: "Status quo bias, effort avoidance"
    response_strategy: |
      1. Cost of waiting (what they lose daily)
      2. Make starting effortless
      3. Show quick time-to-value
      4. Create urgency with deadline

  "need_to_think_about_it":
    real_fear: "I don't have enough information to decide"
    psychology: "Decision anxiety, fear of regret"
    response_strategy: |
      1. Ask what specific concerns they have
      2. Provide the exact information needed
      3. Offer a way to try without committing
      4. Social proof from similar situations

  "need_to_check_with_team":
    real_fear: "I don't want to champion something that fails"
    psychology: "Social risk, career protection"
    response_strategy: |
      1. Provide materials to share
      2. Offer to present to team
      3. Case studies from similar orgs
      4. Make them the hero of the story
