# Multi-Agent Workflow: Weekly Content Engine

> Run a repeatable weekly content pipeline that produces SEO articles, social posts, and a newsletter — without starting from scratch every Monday.

## The Scenario

**Company:** Capsule — a B2B HR software startup. 8-person team, no dedicated marketing hire.
**Problem:** The founding team has domain expertise but no consistent content output. Blog is sporadic. LinkedIn presence is personal, not systematic. The SEO opportunity is real — competitors are ranking for terms they could own.
**Goal:** Build a weekly content machine that runs in under 3 hours of founder time and compounds over time.

## Agent Team

| Agent | Role in this workflow |
|---|---|
| SEO Specialist | Keyword research, topic selection, and optimization briefs |
| Content Creator | Write the long-form article and repurpose it into shorter assets |
| LinkedIn Content Creator | Adapt the core idea into a high-engagement LinkedIn post |
| Social Media Strategist | Twitter/X thread and cross-platform distribution plan |
| Growth Hacker | Newsletter angle and subscriber CTA optimization |

---

## The Workflow

### Monday: Research + Topic Selection (1 hour)

**Step 1 — Activate SEO Specialist**

```
Activate SEO Specialist.

Company: Capsule — HR software for companies with 50-500 employees.
Core product: employee onboarding, performance reviews, and compensation management.
Target audience: HR managers, People Ops leads, CHROs at mid-size companies.
Competitors ranking for our space: Lattice, Rippling, BambooHR.

This week's content focus: [pick one or ask the SEO Specialist to suggest]

Deliver:
1. 3 keyword opportunities under 2,000 monthly searches with <40 KD (low competition, high relevance)
2. The primary keyword and semantic cluster for this week's article
3. What the top-ranking article is missing — this is our angle
4. Title options (pick the one with the highest CTR potential)
5. Suggested internal links from our existing content
```

**Step 2 — Activate Content Creator**

```
Activate Content Creator.

Writing a long-form SEO article for Capsule (HR software).

Keyword brief: [paste SEO Specialist output]

Topic: [chosen title from SEO brief]
Audience: HR managers at 50-500 person companies.
Word count: 1,400-1,800 words.
Tone: practical, direct, peer-to-peer — not corporate, not academic.

Structure:
1. Opening hook — a scenario the reader recognizes from their own week
2. The actual problem this article solves (2-3 paragraphs)
3. The framework or process (3-5 numbered steps with examples)
4. One real example (use Capsule customer context or a realistic composite)
5. Common mistakes section
6. Closing with a specific action the reader can take today

Optimize for: [primary keyword from brief], headers using semantic variants.
Include: meta title (under 60 chars), meta description (under 155 chars), 3 image alt text suggestions.
```

---

### Tuesday–Wednesday: Repurpose Into Social + Newsletter (45 min)

**Step 3 — Activate LinkedIn Content Creator**

```
Activate LinkedIn Content Creator.

Adapting this article into a high-engagement LinkedIn post for the Capsule founder.

Article: [paste Content Creator output]

Author context: founder of Capsule, 8 years in HR tech, writes from personal experience.
Audience: HR leaders, People Ops professionals, startup operators.

Post format: text-only or text + single image.

Requirements:
1. Hook line — first 2 lines must make the reader click "see more" (no "I'm excited to share")
2. The core insight from the article in 150-250 words
3. 3-5 punchy takeaways using line breaks (not bullet points)
4. Closing question to drive comments
5. 3 hashtag options (niche over broad)

Write 2 versions: one personal/story-driven, one data/insight-driven. I'll pick one.
```

**Step 4 — Activate Social Media Strategist**

```
Activate Social Media Strategist.

Repurposing this week's content for Twitter/X and scheduling the full week's distribution.

Article: [paste Content Creator output]
LinkedIn post: [paste LinkedIn Content Creator output]

Deliver:
1. Twitter/X thread: 6-8 tweets. First tweet is the hook, last tweet links to the article.
   Thread should stand alone — don't require clicking the article to get value.
2. Best time to post each platform this week (consider Tuesday–Thursday windows)
3. Engagement tactic: what should I reply to or comment on to amplify reach this week?
   Give me 3 specific posts/threads in the HR space worth engaging with.
4. Repost schedule: when does the LinkedIn post get re-shared (repurpose in 3 weeks)?
```

**Step 5 — Activate Growth Hacker**

```
Activate Growth Hacker.

Writing this week's newsletter section for Capsule's subscriber list (~2,100 HR professionals).

Article: [paste Content Creator output]

Newsletter context:
- Weekly send, Thursday morning
- Format: 3-4 short sections (not a blog dump)
- Existing sections: "This Week in HR" (news), "The Capsule Take" (original insight), "Quick Wins" (tactics)
- Goal: keep the 42% open rate, grow to 3,000 subscribers by Q3

Deliver:
1. "The Capsule Take" section: 200-250 words adapting the article's main insight
   (not a summary — give them the opinion, not the full piece)
2. Subscriber CTA: what's the ask this week? (Not just "read the article" — give them a reason)
3. Subject line: 3 options. Include one curiosity-gap and one direct-benefit version.
4. Preview text (under 85 chars) for each subject line option
```

---

### Thursday: Publish + Schedule

**Publish checklist (founder does this, no agent needed):**
- [ ] Upload article to CMS, publish with meta title/description from brief
- [ ] Schedule LinkedIn post for 8:30am local
- [ ] Queue Twitter/X thread for 9:00am
- [ ] Send newsletter (Thursday morning)
- [ ] Reply to first 5 comments on LinkedIn within 2 hours of posting

---

### Friday: Review + Plan Next Week (15 min)

**Step 6 — Activate SEO Specialist**

```
Activate SEO Specialist.

End-of-week review for Capsule content.

This week's article: [title + URL once published]
LinkedIn post performance: [impressions, likes, comments, shares]
Newsletter: [open rate, click rate, unsubscribes]

Evaluate:
1. Which piece performed best and why?
2. What topic should we double down on next week based on engagement signals?
3. Are there any competitor pieces that published this week we should respond to?
4. Suggest next week's keyword focus based on this week's click data.
```

## Weekly Schedule

| Day | Task | Time | Agent |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | Keyword research + article written | 90 min | SEO Specialist → Content Creator |
| Tuesday | LinkedIn + Twitter adapted | 30 min | LinkedIn Content Creator + Social Media Strategist |
| Wednesday | Newsletter section written | 15 min | Growth Hacker |
| Thursday | Publish + schedule everything | 30 min | Founder (no agent) |
| Friday | Review + plan next week | 15 min | SEO Specialist |

---

## Key Patterns

1. **SEO leads, content follows** — Topic selection is driven by keyword data, not what the founder feels like writing. The SEO Specialist brief is non-negotiable input for the Content Creator.
2. **One article, five assets** — Blog post, LinkedIn post (two versions), Twitter/X thread, newsletter section. One research session, multiple distribution channels.
3. **Social repurposes the idea, not the article** — LinkedIn and Twitter posts are not summaries. They're standalone insights that make the reader want the full piece.
4. **Newsletter is opinion, not curation** — The Growth Hacker's job is to extract the founder's point of view, not recap the article. Subscribers can read the article themselves.
5. **Friday review closes the loop** — Last step feeds the next cycle. Content performance data informs the following week's keyword selection.
6. **Founder time is gated** — Agents do the production work. The founder reviews, selects, and publishes. Max 3 hours per week of founder involvement.

## Tips

- Batch the Monday session — keyword research and article writing in one sitting prevents context-switching tax.
- When the Content Creator produces the article, paste the full output into each subsequent agent. Don't summarize — social variants pick up specific lines and phrases from the original.
- After 8 weeks, you have a content library. Ask the SEO Specialist to identify internal linking opportunities across everything published.
