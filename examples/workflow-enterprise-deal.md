# Multi-Agent Workflow: Enterprise Deal Sprint

> Move a stalled enterprise opportunity from qualification to signed contract using a coordinated sales team.

## The Scenario

**Opportunity:** Meridian Health Systems — a 4,000-person hospital network — has been evaluating VerifyAI for 6 weeks. The champion is the VP of HR. The deal is $72K ARR. It's stalled because IT and Legal haven't been looped in, procurement is asking for a security questionnaire, and a competitor just got a meeting with the CHRO.

Your job: get this deal closed in the next 3 weeks.

## Agent Team

| Agent | Role |
|---|---|
| Discovery Coach | Re-qualify the deal and map the real buying committee |
| Deal Strategist | Build a MEDDPICC-scored win plan |
| Sales Engineer | Own the security questionnaire and technical proof |
| Proposal Strategist | Write the commercial proposal and executive summary |

---

## Phase 1: Re-Qualify and Map the Buying Committee

### Step 1 — Discovery Coach

```
Activate Discovery Coach.

Deal: Meridian Health Systems, $72K ARR, VerifyAI (automated background verification).
Stage: Evaluating for 6 weeks. Stalled.
Champion: Sarah Chen, VP HR.
Problem: IT and Legal not engaged, procurement blocking, competitor in at CHRO level.

Help me prepare for a re-engagement call with Sarah tomorrow.

Questions I need answered on this call:
1. What's changed in the last 6 weeks — has the urgency increased or decreased?
2. Who specifically in IT and Legal needs to approve, and what are their criteria?
3. What does the CHRO care about — is this a cost story, a compliance story, or an efficiency story?
4. What is Sarah's personal stake in getting this done?
5. What does "good" look like for Meridian — what metric would they point to in 6 months?

Give me:
- The exact questions to ask Sarah (open-ended, not leading)
- What each answer tells me about deal health
- The one question that, if I get the right answer, changes everything
```

### Step 2 — Deal Strategist (after call with Sarah)

```
Activate Deal Strategist.

Scoring this deal using MEDDPICC.

What I learned from the re-qualification call:
- Metrics: They process ~1,200 background checks/year. Current vendor costs $85K/year + 3 hours/hire in admin. VerifyAI would cut cost by $35K and time-per-hire by 2 days.
- Economic Buyer: CHRO (Marcus Webb). Sarah has his ear but he needs to see ROI numbers.
- Decision Criteria: HIPAA compliance, SOC 2 Type II, integration with their HRIS (Workday), price.
- Decision Process: IT security review → Legal (BAA required) → Procurement → CHRO sign-off.
- Identify Pain: Confirmed. A bad hire last quarter is driving urgency — Sarah mentioned it twice.
- Champion: Sarah is solid. She wants to own this win. She'll set up the CHRO meeting if I give her the right ammunition.
- Competition: Checkr is in at the CHRO level. Meeting happened 2 weeks ago. Unknown outcome.
- Paper Process: 3-4 weeks after verbal approval. Q1 budget still has room.

Deliver:
1. MEDDPICC score (1-5 per pillar) with commentary
2. The 2-3 things that will kill this deal if unaddressed
3. A 3-week action plan: what needs to happen each week for this to close
4. How to neutralize Checkr — what we have that they don't, and how to position it
5. The ask for Sarah: what do I need her to do this week?
```

## Phase 2: Unblock IT and Legal

### Step 3 — Sales Engineer

```
Activate Sales Engineer.

Deal: Meridian Health Systems — healthcare organization, 4,000 employees.
Blocker: IT security review + Legal needs a BAA (Business Associate Agreement).

Two deliverables needed:

1. Security questionnaire response
Meridian IT sent a 40-question security questionnaire. Key areas:
- Data residency (they need US-only storage)
- Encryption (at rest and in transit)
- Access controls and audit logging
- Penetration testing cadence
- Incident response SLA
- HIPAA compliance documentation
- SOC 2 Type II report availability

Draft professional responses to each category. Where we have strong answers, be specific. Where we have gaps, draft what we can say honestly without overpromising.

2. BAA talking points
Legal needs to negotiate the Business Associate Agreement. They'll push on:
- Liability caps
- Breach notification timelines
- Subprocessor list and controls
- Data retention and deletion

Give me the 5 positions we should hold firm on and the 3 areas where we can move.
```

---

## Phase 3: The CHRO Meeting

### Step 4 — Discovery Coach (prep for executive meeting)

```
Activate Discovery Coach.

I have a 30-minute meeting with Marcus Webb, CHRO at Meridian Health Systems.
Sarah (VP HR) set it up. Checkr already had a meeting with him.

Marcus's likely priorities:
- Risk reduction (compliance, liability)
- Cost efficiency
- Operational excellence

I need to walk in with a sharp executive conversation, not a product demo.

Give me:
- The 2 questions to open with (understand his frame before presenting)
- The 60-second business case tailored to a CHRO at a healthcare organization
- How to address Checkr directly if he brings them up (without badmouthing)
- The close: what's the specific ask at the end of a 30-minute executive meeting?
```

### Step 5 — Proposal Strategist (runs in parallel with CHRO prep)

```
Activate Proposal Strategist.

Build the commercial proposal for Meridian Health Systems.

Deal context:
- Product: VerifyAI
- Value: Save $35K/year vs current vendor, reduce time-per-hire by 2 days, eliminate manual admin
- Term: 12 months, $72K ARR ($6K/month)
- Special terms: 30-day pilot period, SOC 2 report provided on signature, BAA included

Proposal must contain:
1. Executive summary (1 page) — written for the CHRO, not the VP HR. ROI-first.
2. The problem we're solving — use Meridian's specific numbers, not generic claims
3. How VerifyAI works — 3 steps, no jargon
4. Why now — what's the cost of a 3-month delay? Calculate it.
5. Commercial terms — clear pricing, what's included, implementation timeline
6. Risk reversal — the pilot structure, success criteria, what happens if they're not satisfied
7. Next steps — specific dates and owners

Tone: confident, direct, no filler. Marcus reads dozens of proposals. This one needs to get to the point in the first paragraph.
```

## Phase 3: Close

### Step 6 — Deal Strategist (final push)

```
Activate Deal Strategist.

Week 3 of the Meridian deal. Status update:
- CHRO meeting went well — Marcus said "send me the proposal"
- IT security questionnaire submitted, 2 open questions remaining
- Legal has the BAA — their counsel is reviewing
- Checkr apparently proposed at $65K (Sarah told me)
- Procurement needs a W-9 and insurance certificate

Action needed: Final close plan for the next 10 days.

Give me:
1. How to respond to the Checkr price — hold at $72K or move?
2. How to accelerate Legal review without being pushy
3. What to do if IT comes back with a blocking issue we can't answer
4. The sequence of touches this week — who gets what communication and when
5. The "mutual close plan" I send to Sarah: a shared document with dates, owners, and what happens when
```

## Key Patterns

**Re-qualify before strategizing** — Discovery Coach runs first. There's no point building a win plan on stale assumptions. One call with the champion changes everything.

**Parallel unblocking** — Sales Engineer works on IT/Legal while Deal Strategist builds the CHRO narrative. Both blockers need to be removed simultaneously.

**Executive meeting is not a demo** — Discovery Coach runs again before the CHRO meeting. Senior buyers want a conversation, not a presentation.

**Proposal follows the meeting, not before** — The proposal is written with CHRO framing in mind after the meeting, not before. Proposals built on assumptions get ignored.

**Competitive response is last** — Deal Strategist addresses Checkr after everything else is in motion. Chasing competitor intel too early distracts from the real work.
